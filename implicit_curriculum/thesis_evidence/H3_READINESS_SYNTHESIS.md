# H3 readiness synthesis

Family 2 exposed an important H3 failure mode: H2 can select composites with very large residuals that are too hard for intervention contrasts. This does not refute exact-component dependency; it means the candidate is outside the measurable H3 regime.

The thesis claim should therefore distinguish:

- **Residual selection:** H2 identifies candidate composites where the atomic parallel-rate model fails.
- **H3 readiness:** a candidate must have nontrivial partial acquisition or final performance so interventions can move it.
- **H3 evidence:** exact-component interventions must beat same-operation and different-operation controls.

The readiness-aware H3 workflow prevents a methodological error: treating an unlearnable composite as a negative dependency result. A hard-composite failure is evidence about task difficulty and candidate selection, not evidence against dependency.
